01  |  Project Background Problem Background Online food delivery platforms have surged globally (Huang & Siao 2023), yet third-party commissions of  20–35% per order  (Chan et al. 2023) and depleted profit margins (Sharffudin & Yadav 2025) leave small food businesses financially strained and unable to build direct customer loyalty. 20–35% Commission per order (Chan et al. 2023) 0% Commission with BizMenu Builder 100% Customer data ownership retained Project Objectives Branded profiles with custom logos and colour schemes Live menu management with real-time price and availability updates Commission-free customer ordering via branded storefront Instant order notifications and full ownership of customer data Stakeholders & Significance 🏪 Primary: Small food business owners — financial independence, data control 👥 Secondary: Customers — smooth, branded, commission-free ordering experience 🌏 Context: Targets digital inequity in Australian food service SMEs INF305 — BizMenu Builder  |  Mid-Point Report  |  Semester 1, 2026

02  |  Literature Review Existing Solutions & Identified Gaps Uber Eats / DoorDash Multi-sided digital marketplaces. High commissions (20–35%) undermine small restaurant profitability (Dai & Wu 2023). GloriaFood / ChowNow / UpMenu White-label monthly subscriptions. Require technical skills most SME owners lack (Ocloo et al. 2024). BizMenu Builder Gap No existing solution offers: zero commission + full data isolation + real-time menu + free prototype — all in one lightweight platform. Technology Stack & Justifications Next.js & React.js: full-stack framework (no separate Express server needed) MongoDB Atlas: Document-oriented NoSQL — dynamic menu structures without rigid schema (Banker et al. 2016). Vercel: Cloud hosting eliminates local server costs — critical for small operators (Thipparthy et al. 2024). INF305 — BizMenu Builder  |  Mid-Point Report  |  Semester 1, 2026

06  |  Risk Management Risk Category Potential Risk Impact Mitigation Strategy Technical Risk Incorrect import paths / missing dependencies High Code validation and testing before deployment; proper file structure (Lehmann & Recker 2022) Database Risk MongoDB connection failure High Correct environment variables; test connectivity regularly Deployment Risk Vercel build or deployment errors High Monitor build logs; immediate fix before redeployment Security Risk Exposure of sensitive data (DB URI) High Store credentials in .env files; restrict public access Performance Risk Slow system response or delays Medium Optimise queries; improve backend performance User Risk Difficulty in using the interface Medium Intuitive UI design; simple navigation Network Risk DNS misconfiguration Medium Verify DNS records; ensure correct domain configuration Maintenance Risk Updates causing bugs Low Version control and staged testing before release INF305 —  BizMenu  Builder
