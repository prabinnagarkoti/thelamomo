ASSESSMENT BRIEF  
 
At the midpoint of the capstone project, your group is required to demonstrate the progress on your project. 
This stage is essential to ensure that your work is on track and continues to align with the objectives approved 
in your project proposal.  
You are expected to provide evidence of iterative development, the effective use of Scrum practices, and an 
early implementation of your prototype.  
The midpoint assessment ensures that the project is on track, risks are identified, and corrective actions can 
be applied if needed.  
 
Mid Report should include:  
• Project Background (problem background, Objectives of the project, Stakeholders and significance 
of the project)  
• Literature Review (Analysis of existing solutions, technologies, and methods, justifications for 
selections)  
• Design (Architecture diagrams, Database models, Wireframes / UI prototypes)  
• Prototype (Features implemented, Screenshots)  
• Product Backlog & Sprint Review Minutes (user stories, epics, acceptance criteria, priority, status, 
Sprint review minutes with decisions, issues, and action items)  
• Risk Management (Issues encountered and solutions, Potential risks and mitigation strategies)  
• Next Plan (Planned for upcoming sprints)  
• References  
• Appendix (Data collection instruments, Gantt chart, work breakdown structure, any additional 
supporting materials)  
Submission Components:  
• Mid-report document  
• Demo video of the partially implemented prototype.  
• Presentation slides  
 
As part of this assessment, your group will deliver an in -class presentation and demo lasting 20 minutes, 
which includes a Q&A session.  
All group members are required to actively participate and demonstrate their respective contributions to the 
project.  
Students will be awarded marks for both group contributions and individual contributions.
